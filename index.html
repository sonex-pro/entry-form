BATTS Open 1-Star Tournament
Sunday 2nd November 2025
Sponsored by Bribar

Welcome to the BATTS Open 1-Star Tournament entry form.

This form is quick and paperless, but please:

  • Read all details carefully before starting
  • Make sure you press SUBMIT at the end
  • Complete payment after submission to confirm your entry

Event details:

  • Approved by Table Tennis England and Essex County TTA
  • Date: Sunday, 2nd November 2025
  • Venue: BATTS TT Club, Norman Booth Centre, Elderfield, Mulberry Green, Old Harlow, Essex CM17 0EY
  • Referee: Neil Brierley (Club Manager)
  • Organiser: Carl Johnson (TTE Level 1 coach) – Tel: 07469 844024, Email: carl.johnson.batts@gmail.com

Prize fund:

  • Band 1 Winner £250, Runner Up £120, Semi-finalists £50 each
  • Band 2 Winner £50, Runner Up £40
  • Band 3 Winner £40, Runner Up £30

Entry fee: £35 (£30 BATTS fee + £5 umpire fee, refunded if you umpire at the knockout stage)

Maximum 48 entries accepted in order of receipt and payment.

The tournament runs all day (approx. 9:15am to 5:30pm), players must be available for morning and afternoon sessions, venue opens at 8:00am for registration & practice (play starts 9:15am).

Under 18s must be accompanied by an adult, the draw will be made on 31st October but not published in advance.

New to tournaments? Visit Table Tennis England Start Competing.

  • 1. Every entrant must be affiliated as a Compete Member or provide proof of being a member of another National Association in membership of the ITTF.
  • 2. An entrant who is not a TTE member or is affiliated as a Supporter or Club Play Member may alternately purchase a One-Off Tournament Pass at the time of entry via https://tabletennisengland.sport80.com/  It is not possible to obtain the OTTP at the tournament
  • 3. TTE Regulations Part A (Regulated Competitions) and Part B (Tournament Regulations) apply to the tournament.
  • 4. ITTF Regulations for International Competitions apply to the tournament except where otherwise specified in TTE Regulations Parts A or B, or in this form.
  • 5. Completion and submission of this Entry Form signifies agreement by the entrant to the conditions of the competition, including any variations of ITTF regulations.
  • 6. Entries must be submitted with full payment of fees.
  • 7. Playing clothing shall consist of a shirt and shorts, trousers, leggings or skirt, or a one-part sports outfit, socks and playing shoes; a sports hijab may also be worn, other garments shall not be worn during play except with the permission of the referee, ITTF 3.2.2.1 amended.
  • 8. The main colour of a shirt, shorts, trousers or skirt, other than sleeves and collar of a shirt, shall be clearly different from that of the ball in use, ITTF 3.2.2.2 amended markings or trimming on the front or side of a playing garment and objects such as jewellery worn by a player shall not be so conspicuous or brightly reflecting as to unsight an opponent, ITTF 3.2.2.5.
  • 9. No competitor or official shall engage in betting on players or matches.
  • 10. Doping shall not take place either before or during play, but doping control tests may take place.
  • 11. Completion and submission of this Entry Form signifies agreement by the entrant to the conditions of the competition, including any variations of ITTF regulations, full payment at the time of registration will be required to secure your entry, entries received with no payment will not be entered.
  • 12. Playing conditions Play will be on 8 Tibhar tables with Tibhar posts and nets, tournament balls will be Tibhar 40+ 3star ITTF approved, score indicators will be used for all matches, each table will be in an individual court enclosed by surrounds, each court will be 4.7 metres wide by 9.9 metres in length, minimum height of playing area is 8 metres, minimum height of lighting from floor is 8 metres, lighting is general LED, flooring is semi-sprung timber.
  • 13. Practice on the 8 tournament tables are allowed from 8:15am to 9:15am after 9:15am no practice is available, cross knocking is advised, and limited to 15 minutes when someone is waiting.
  • 14. No entry will be accepted after the draw has been made, subject to the discretion of the Referee, the draw will also be close to but not entirely in rank order.
  • 15. All entries and event details are subject to COVID-related restrictions, or protocols that Table Tennis England may deem necessary, venues used for events may impose COVID restrictions that go beyond government guidance.

Tournament Regulations Continued

  • 16. All competitors are required to umpire in their respected group, umpiring will be required in the knockout rounds to help ensure the tournament finishes on time.
  • 17. Each match will consist of best of 5 games, each game up to 11 points with a 2 point difference to decide the winner, all players will compete in a group of 6 ( unless there are player absences ), all players will advance to the knock out banding stage, Band 1, Band 2 and Band 3.
